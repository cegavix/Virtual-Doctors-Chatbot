User: What are the symptoms of chickenpox?
Assistant: The most common symptom of chickenpox is an itchy, blister-like rash that appears in waves over several days. Other symptoms may include fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, and headache.
Assistant: Chickenpox is highly contagious and can spread easily through contact with the varicella-zoster virus, which is airborne through coughs and sneezes, or by direct contact with the rash.
Assistant: Chickenpox usually lasts about 5-7 days, with the itching and discomfort peaking around days 3-5. The scabs from the rash can take up to two weeks to disappear completely.
Assistant: There is no specific cure for chickenpox, but home care can help manage symptoms. This includes getting plenty of rest, staying hydrated, using calamine lotion to relieve itching, and taking over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen for fever and discomfort. Avoid scratching the blisters to prevent scarring.
